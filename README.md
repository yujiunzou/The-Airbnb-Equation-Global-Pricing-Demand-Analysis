# The-AirBnB-Equation  
## Airbnb Pricing & Review Analysis: NYC vs London

---

## Project Overview
Airbnb is one of the worldâ€™s largest platforms for short-term vacation rentals. As the number of listings grows, it becomes increasingly difficult for guests to choose suitable accommodations and for hosts to compete effectively.

This project analyzes Airbnb booking and hosting activity across different locations to understand what drives pricing, demand, and guest satisfaction. We focus on two major global travel destinations: **New York City** and **London**.

---

## Motivation
As frequent travelers, we often question whether an Airbnb booking is worth the price or risk. This motivated us to explore what makes certain listings more successful than others.

By identifying the key drivers behind pricing, demand, and reviews, this analysis aims to help hosts make better business decisions and help guests better evaluate listings.

---

## Data Source
- **Inside Airbnb** (public datasets)
- Listings and reviews data for:
  - New York City
  - London

### Dataset Size
- NYC listings: 36,289  
- NYC reviews: 986,598  
- London listings: 96,995  
- London reviews: 2,019,218  

---

## Key Questions
- Which factors influence Airbnb pricing the most?
- How do price and demand differ across cities and neighborhoods?
- How do holidays and seasonal trends affect pricing and demand?
- What drives positive or negative guest reviews?

---

## Methodology
- Data cleaning and preprocessing using SQL  
- LEFT JOIN listings and reviews on `listing_id`  
- Aggregation and exploratory analysis  
- Visualization using interactive dashboards  

---

## Key Insights
- NYC listings are significantly more expensive, with larger price variability  
- London has a larger supply, leading to more competitive pricing  
- Location is the strongest driver of price in both cities  
- Seasonal effects increase demand but do not drastically widen price gaps  
- Certain neighborhoods present opportunity zones for hosts  

---

## Outcome
This analysis highlights actionable patterns that can support better decision-making for both Airbnb hosts and travelers.
